Navient Sued for Student Loan Relief



By Colin McNamara
April 20, 2023

Navient Corporation, one of the largest federal student loan providers, has faced an increasing number of lawsuits since 2017. A group of 39 attorneys general announced a major settlement on January 13, 2022 that required Navient to provide $1.85 billion in relief to student loan borrowers.

In addition, Navient will be forced to pay $260 to approximately 350,00 federal loan borrowers who were advised to accept a long-term forbearance instead of an income-driven repayment, totaling $95 million.

Student loan complaints against Navient skyrocketed to 3,828 in January of 2017, based on data from the Consumer Protection Financial Bureau (CPFB). There were a total of 63,660 complaints filed from December 2011 through April 2023. Nearly 50% were based on student loans, with debt collection, mortgage and credit also receiving a substantial amount of complaints, according to the CPFB.
